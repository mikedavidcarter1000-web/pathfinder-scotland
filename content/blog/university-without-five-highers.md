"You need 5 Highers for university." It's the line every Scottish student has heard. **It's also wrong.**

Several Scottish universities accept three Highers for direct entry. Most accept four. And there are at least six well-established alternative routes that bypass the Highers count entirely.

This guide walks through every realistic path into a Scottish university for someone who doesn't have five A-grade Highers — whether that's because of academic performance, life circumstances, or simply being a few years out of school.

## Route 1 — Direct entry with fewer Highers

The cleanest route, and one many students don't realise is open to them.

| University | Course examples | Entry requirements |
|------------|------------------|---------------------|
| **Abertay** | Business, computing, game design | ABB to BBC across 3 Highers |
| **University of the West of Scotland** | Most courses | CCC, or CC plus a widening access programme |
| **Glasgow Caledonian** | Nursing, computing, business | BCC to CCC |
| **University of Aberdeen** | Multiple subjects | BBC guaranteed under widening access |
| **Edinburgh Napier** | Nursing, computing | BBC for nursing, BBBC for many others |

Even at the more competitive universities, contextual offers based on widening access can drop the standard requirement by 1 to 2 grades.

## Route 2 — College then university (HNC and HND articulation)

Probably the single most effective alternative route, and badly underused.

You start at a Scottish college on a Higher National Certificate (1 year, SCQF Level 7) or Higher National Diploma (2 years, SCQF Level 8). At the end you **articulate** — transfer — into Year 2 or Year 3 of a degree at a Scottish university.

The degree at the end is exactly the same. Your transcript shows the same university, the same course, and the same final classification as students who entered through traditional routes.

**Entry requirements for the HNC or HND** are typically much lower than for a degree — often 1 to 2 Highers at C, or equivalent.

The strongest articulation routes:

- **Glasgow Caledonian** (GCU Pathways from BB — 2 Highers)
- **University of the West of Scotland**
- **Robert Gordon University**
- **Edinburgh Napier**
- **Abertay**
- **University of Stirling**

Many HNC and HND courses are also available **fully online** — Glasgow Clyde College's HNC Computing is the standard example. Funded through college bursaries, which usually cover both fees and a maintenance grant.

For more on the financial side, see our [cost of university guide](/blog/cost-of-university-scotland).

## Route 3 — SWAP (Scottish Wider Access Programme)

Designed for adults three or more years out of formal education with few or no qualifications.

[SWAP](https://www.scottishwideraccess.org/) runs a one-year Access programme at a partner college. Partner universities **guarantee Year 1 entry** to students who pass the Access course at the required standard.

SWAPEast (covering Edinburgh, Lothians, Borders, and Fife) alone has nearly 1,000 documented progression routes into degrees — covering medicine, nursing, social work, engineering, business, and the arts. SWAPWest covers Glasgow and the west.

Funding is through the college bursary system. The three-year out-of-education rule is sometimes waived for care-experienced students, refugees, and asylum seekers.

## Route 4 — Foundation Apprenticeship plus Highers

A Foundation Apprenticeship (SCQF Level 6) counts as a Higher at every Scottish university. So if you have an FA plus four Highers, you have five Higher-level qualifications.

In practice this means an FA + ABBB is treated as equivalent to AABBB at universities including Glasgow and Edinburgh — opening up courses that look out of reach on a four-Higher transcript alone.

The full guide is at [What is a Foundation Apprenticeship?](/blog/foundation-apprenticeship-guide).

## Route 5 — University access courses and summer schools

Most Scottish universities run their own access programmes for students who qualify on widening access criteria.

- **Glasgow Top-Up / Summer School** — guaranteed BBBB offer to participants
- **St Andrews Gateway** — BBBB offer with interview
- **Aberdeen Access to Degree Studies**
- **Dundee Access Summer School**
- **Strathclyde STAMS** — for medicine specifically

Each programme has its own eligibility criteria — usually a combination of school attended (SHEP schools), postcode (SIMD20), and personal circumstances (care-experienced, young carer, first in family). For a full breakdown of eligibility, see [What does SIMD mean and why does it matter for university?](/blog/simd-explained) and our [widening access guide](/blog/widening-access-guide).

## Route 6 — Open University

The [Open University in Scotland](https://www.open.ac.uk/scotland/) requires no formal qualifications for most undergraduate courses. You can study part-time alongside work, from anywhere in Scotland, with full SAAS support if you qualify.

Particularly suitable for adults with caring responsibilities, those in rural areas, and people who need to keep earning while they study. Around 12,000 Scottish students are enrolled with the OU at any given time.

## Which route fits your situation?

| Your situation | Best route |
|----------------|------------|
| School leaver with 3 Highers | Direct entry to Abertay / UWS / GCU / Aberdeen |
| School leaver with 1 to 2 Highers | HNC then articulate into Year 2 |
| Adult, no qualifications | SWAP |
| Adult wanting flexibility | Open University or online HNC |
| Disadvantaged background | University access programme + contextual offer |
| Currently in S5 or S6 | Foundation Apprenticeship + Highers |

There is no single "right" route. The best route is the one that fits your circumstances, your finances, and your tolerance for the time it takes.

## What employers actually care about

This is the bit nobody says. Employers care about your degree and your skills — not how many Highers you had at 17.

A graduate from a Scottish university articulating in via HNC has the same degree as one who got in directly with five Highers. They went to the same lectures, sat the same exams, and got the same parchment at the end. Employers know this. Most don't even ask.

If you're worried about being a "second-class graduate" because you took the longer route, please don't be. The route is invisible by the time you're applying for jobs.

## What to do next

1. **Be honest about your current grades.** Map them against direct-entry options at Abertay, UWS, GCU, and Aberdeen.
2. **If your grades fall short, look at HNC articulation.** It's the single most effective route into a Scottish university for someone who doesn't have a strong Higher transcript.
3. **Check widening access eligibility.** It can shift your entry requirements significantly. Use the [widening access tool](/widening-access).
4. **Talk to your guidance teacher or your local college.** Both have admissions staff whose job is to help you find a route that works.

The myth that you need five Highers for university stops more Scottish students applying than any other single belief. It's not true. There are at least six well-established alternative routes into a Scottish degree — and the qualification at the end is the same.
