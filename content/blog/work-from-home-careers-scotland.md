Around **49 percent of Scottish workers** now work from home at least some of the time, according to the [CIPD Working Lives Scotland 2025 report](https://www.cipd.org/uk/views-and-insights/cipd-voice/working-lives-scotland/). But only 7 percent work fully from home. 42 percent are hybrid (splitting their week between home and office) and 51 percent never work from home at all.

After a steady drift back to offices since 2021, the pattern has now stabilised. Hybrid working isn't going away, but fully remote roles remain scarce — particularly at entry level. If you're considering a career partly because you'd like to work from home, the honest picture is closer to "two days a week at home" than "anywhere in the world with WiFi".

This guide breaks down which Scottish sectors actually offer remote and hybrid work, which don't, and what young workers in particular need to know.

## Where in Scotland is remote work most common?

The geography matters more than people realise. Edinburgh leads with 35 percent of workers mainly working from home, reflecting its concentration of financial services and tech firms. Glasgow follows at 24 percent, Dundee at 23 percent, and Aberdeen at just 18 percent — the legacy of an oil and gas economy where most jobs are physically tied to a site.

Dunfermline was recently named the best city in the UK for remote workers — fast trains to Edinburgh, lower rent, gigabit broadband, and a critical mass of remote-first employers.

If you live somewhere outside the central belt and don't want to relocate, it's worth knowing that the proportion of fully remote jobs is small — but the proportion of hybrid roles where you're in the office two or three days a week is rising fast across all four major cities.

## The entry-level reality

Here's the bit that's rarely said clearly. Only around 28 percent of entry-level jobs offer any flexibility — 18 percent hybrid, 10 percent remote — compared to 43 percent of senior roles. Workers with degrees are roughly 10 times more likely to work hybrid than those without qualifications.

Translation: if you're 18 and starting your first job, fully remote is unusual. Hybrid is realistic. And you'll usually have to earn the option after a year or two of proving yourself in person.

## Careers ranked by realistic remote and hybrid availability

The honest tiering looks like this.

### Tier 1 — Strong remote or hybrid access, including at entry level

**Computing, software and technology.** The most reliably remote-friendly sector. Scotland has major tech hubs in Edinburgh (fintech — over 250 fintech firms) and Glasgow (games, digital). Entry-level access through [CodeClan](https://codeclan.com/) (93 percent employment rate within 6 months), Foundation Apprenticeships in Software Development, or Modern Apprenticeships. Most junior developer roles are hybrid (2–3 days in office) rather than fully remote.

**Customer service and call centres.** One of the most accessible fully remote sectors. Scottish Water, Lloyds Banking Group, and a range of financial services firms hire remote customer service roles often requiring no degree.

**Digital marketing, copywriting, and SEO.** Can be done remotely with portfolios and free certifications from [Google Digital Garage](https://learndigital.withgoogle.com/digitalgarage) or HubSpot Academy.

### Tier 2 — Mostly hybrid, fully remote only with experience

**Financial services.** Edinburgh is the UK's second-largest financial centre. Most analyst, accounting, and operations roles are 2–3 days in the office.

**Public sector.** Scottish Water is a standout — Top 10 Flexible Employer with 60 percent of roles "fluid". The [Scottish Government](https://www.gov.scot/) has hybrid working and a right to disconnect policy. Most central government and local authority desk roles fall here.

**Business and finance.** HR, project management, and general business operations.

### Tier 3 — Limited remote options

**Engineering design.** Some CAD and modelling work can be remote, but site visits and lab access usually mean hybrid at most.

**Creative and design.** Studio collaboration is still the norm in most agencies, though freelancers can work fully remote.

**Science.** Data analysis can be remote. Lab work cannot.

### Tier 4 — Minimal remote work

Healthcare (clinical), education, hospitality, retail, construction, trades, agriculture, emergency services. These are physical-presence jobs by definition. If working from home is a deal-breaker for you, these are not your sectors.

## Careers you can start from home without a degree

If you don't have Highers, or you have Highers but don't want to go to university, these are the realistic remote and hybrid entry points.

| Role | Typical entry salary | Route |
|------|----------------------|-------|
| Customer service representative | £22,000 to £28,000 | Apply directly — often no qualifications required |
| Digital marketing assistant | £20,000 to £24,000 | Free certifications + portfolio |

These figures are based on 2026 Scottish job postings and should be treated as ranges, not guarantees.

## What nobody tells you about working from home at 18

The advantages of remote work are real. So are the disadvantages — and they fall harder on young workers than on anyone else.

A [Harvard Business School study](https://www.hbs.edu/faculty/Pages/item.aspx?num=63148) found software engineers near teammates received 18 percent more feedback than those working remotely, with the gains concentrated among younger workers. Young engineers under 30 were five times more likely to quit when moved to fully remote.

A separate study tracking promotions over five years found remote workers were promoted 31 percent less frequently than in-office colleagues — not because of lower performance, but because of "proximity bias" (managers promote the people they see).

And on the personal side: 57 percent of young workers in a 2024 Deloitte survey said they were concerned about missing social connections if they worked remotely.

## Hybrid is the sweet spot

The good news: hybrid working — typically two days at home, three in the office — captures most of the benefit of remote work without the career penalty.

A major study published in [Nature](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-024-08114-4) found hybrid working had zero negative effect on productivity or career advancement, while quit rates fell by 33 percent.

74 percent of Gen Z workers in the same survey said they preferred face-to-face colleague interactions for at least part of the week. Only 26 percent wanted fully remote work.

The honest takeaway: aim for a hybrid-friendly career rather than a fully remote one. You'll be happier, learn more, and progress faster.

## The practical requirements

If you do work remotely or hybrid, the basics matter.

**Broadband.** 82 percent of Scottish premises have gigabit-capable access. The [Scottish Broadband Voucher Scheme](https://www.gov.scot/publications/digital-scotland-superfast-broadband-voucher-scheme/) provides up to £5,000 for rural areas without it.

**A dedicated workspace.** A desk in a quiet room. Working from a kitchen table for nine hours a day breaks down quickly.

**Self-discipline.** This is the one nobody warns you about. If you can't structure your own day without a manager standing nearby, fully remote will not work for you. Hybrid is a safer bet while you build the habit.

## What to do next

1. **Be honest about what you actually want.** "Working from home sometimes" and "working from home always" are completely different career goals.
2. **Aim for hybrid-friendly sectors at entry level.** Tech, financial services, and public sector are the realistic targets in Scotland.
3. **Don't write off the office.** The first two years of any career are when you learn the most — usually from people sitting near you.
4. **Check your local broadband.** If you're rural, the [Scottish Broadband Voucher Scheme](https://www.gov.scot/publications/digital-scotland-superfast-broadband-voucher-scheme/) is real money on the table.

The future of Scottish work is hybrid. Build a career around that, not around the YouTube fantasy of working from a beach in Bali.
