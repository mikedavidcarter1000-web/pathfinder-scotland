The Scottish Qualifications Authority is gone. **Qualifications Scotland** is its replacement. The new body became a legal entity on 1 December 2025, became fully operational on 1 February 2026, and the SQA was formally dissolved on 26 February 2026 — established by the [Education (Scotland) Act 2025](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/asp/2025).

If you're sitting Highers or National 5s in 2026, this is the one-line version: **nothing about your exams changes.** The body that runs them has a new name. The exams, the courses, and the way you'll be assessed are all the same.

This guide is the slightly longer version, in plain language, of what's actually changed.

## Why the SQA was replaced

Heavy criticism during COVID — particularly the [2020 exam results algorithm](https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-scotland-53745613) that downgraded thousands of students disproportionately from disadvantaged areas — destroyed public trust in the SQA. The Scottish Government announced a replacement in June 2021. The independent Hayward Review followed and recommended a fresh organisation with a new structure and a different relationship with learners and teachers.

The Education (Scotland) Act 2025 enacted that recommendation. Qualifications Scotland is the result.

## What has not changed (the important bit for current students)

If you're in S4, S5, or S6 right now, here's what stays the same.

- **2026 exams go ahead as planned.** The diet runs from 22 April to 1 June 2026. Results day is 4 August 2026.
- **Courses, content, and assessment methods are unchanged.** Your Highers and Advanced Highers are taught and assessed exactly as they were last year.
- **Highers, Advanced Highers, and National 5s still exist** with the same names and the same SCQF levels.
- **Your school's approved centre status continues.** Schools don't have to reapply or relicense.
- **Past qualifications remain valid.** A Higher you sat in 2023 is still a Higher. A degree built on previous SQA qualifications is still a degree.
- **Your teachers' contacts are unchanged.** The same people they emailed at the SQA still work at Qualifications Scotland.

In short: the building got a new sign over the door. Nobody moved out.

## What has changed

- **The name and brand.** Websites are rebranding through 2026. Emails are moving to `@qualifications.gov.scot` but old SQA addresses still work.
- **A new Board.** 13 members including headteachers and college staff — a deliberately different composition from the old SQA Board.
- **New committees and charters.** Learner and practitioner committees are being established, along with public-facing charters that set out what students and teachers can expect from the organisation. This is the structural fix the Hayward Review pushed for.

## What is coming over the next few years

The big curriculum changes are slower. The published timeline:

| Year | Change |
|------|--------|
| 2026 | Assessment rebalancing begins — more continuous assessment, exams carry slightly less weight |
| 2027 | Revised curriculum guidance published |
| 2028 (August) | New curriculum implemented for S1 to S3 |
| 2029 (August) | New curriculum extended to S4 to S6 |
| 2031 | Reformed National 4 / National 5 qualifications |
| 2032 | New Higher and Advanced Higher |

If you're in S1 or S2 right now, you'll be the cohort that experiences the new curriculum from S1 onwards. If you're in S3 or above, the changes mostly happen around you, not to you.

## What this means for university applications

Nothing changes for 2026 or 2027 applicants. UCAS tariff points are unchanged. Foundation Apprenticeships are unchanged. Universities continue to accept Highers and Advanced Highers exactly as before — they're now issued by Qualifications Scotland rather than the SQA, but that's an administrative detail.

## Who to talk to if you have questions

- **Your school's guidance team** for anything specific to your exams or coursework.
- **[Qualifications Scotland](https://www.qualifications.gov.scot/)** for official guidance — replaces sqa.org.uk over the course of 2026.
- **[SAAS](https://www.saas.gov.uk/)** for student finance once you're applying to university.
- **[UCAS](https://www.ucas.com/)** for the application process itself.

## What to do next

1. **Don't panic.** Your 2026 exams are exactly as planned. Nothing about your subjects, your timetable, or your assessment has changed.
2. **Keep using the same study resources.** Past papers from the SQA era are still valid practice — same syllabus, same standard.
3. **Bookmark [qualifications.gov.scot](https://www.qualifications.gov.scot/)** for any official communications going forward.
4. **If you're in S1 or S2, check back closer to 2028** — that's when the new curriculum starts to affect your year group.

The transition from SQA to Qualifications Scotland is the biggest structural change to Scottish qualifications in a generation, but it's been deliberately designed to happen around current students, not to them. Your 2026 exams are safe. Your past results are safe. The new body is the same teachers, the same examiners, and the same exam papers — under a different name and with a different governance structure intended to avoid another 2020.
