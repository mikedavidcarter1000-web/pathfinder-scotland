Business is one of the most flexible degree areas in Scotland. The subject requirements depend on which strand you pick.

- **Business Management & Marketing** — few specific subject requirements, 3–5 Highers.
- **Accounting & Finance** — Higher Maths usually needed.
- **Economics** — Higher Maths essential.

## Business Management entry across Scotland

### University of Glasgow

Standard **AAABB**, WA **ABBBB** guaranteed. English at B.

### University of Edinburgh

Standard **AAAB**, WA **AABB**.

### University of Strathclyde

Standard **AABB–AAAB**, WA **ABBB–AABB**. Essay-based Highers preferred. Strathclyde Business School is the largest in Scotland and AACSB-accredited.

### University of Aberdeen

Standard **BBBB**, WA **BBC** guaranteed unconditional.

### University of Dundee

Standard **AABB**, WA **BBBC**.

### University of Stirling

Standard **ABBB**, WA **BBBC**.

### Heriot-Watt

Standard **AABB**, WA **BBBC** guaranteed.

### Edinburgh Napier

Standard **BBBC**, WA **BCCC**.

### Glasgow Caledonian

Standard **ABBB**, WA **BCCC**.

### Robert Gordon University

Standard **BBBB**, WA **BCCC**.

### Abertay

Standard **BBBC**, WA **BBC** (3 Highers).

### UWS

Standard **BBBB**, WA **CCC** (3 Highers).

## Best Highers for flexibility

The combination that opens every business degree at every Scottish university:

- **English**
- **Maths**
- **Business Management** (or any social science)
- Two others

Maths is the gatekeeper if you might pivot into Accounting, Finance, or Economics later.

## The college route — strongest in business

Business is the area where the college articulation route is most established. Almost every Scottish college offers an HNC or HND Business.

- **GCU Pathways** — articulate from BB (2 Highers).
- **HND Business** — can lead directly to **Year 3 entry** at most modern universities.

If your Highers don't quite hit the entry requirement for the university you want, the HNC / HND route can put you on track within a year.

## Professional accreditation matters for Accounting

If you're heading for chartered accountancy, the professional bodies are:

- **ICAS** — Institute of Chartered Accountants of Scotland
- **ACCA** — Association of Chartered Certified Accountants
- **CIMA** — Chartered Institute of Management Accountants

Glasgow, Strathclyde, GCU, and Stirling offer the maximum **ICAS exemptions** — meaning your degree counts towards the chartered qualification and shortens your training contract.

## What to do next

1. **Decide on the strand.** Marketing, Accounting, and Economics need very different Highers.
2. **Lock in Maths if you might pivot.** It's the most useful insurance subject.
3. **Look at the placement / sandwich year.** Strathclyde, Stirling, and Heriot-Watt all have strong industry connections.
