In S5, you choose exactly 5 subjects. That's down from 6–8 in S4 — a sharp narrowing. Most students sit 3–5 Highers, with the rest being National 5s they're picking up or improving. There are no compulsory subjects in S5 (unlike S4 where English and Maths are mandatory), so for the first time, every choice is yours.

Your school uses a column system — 5 columns, one subject per column. You can't pick two subjects in the same column, even if you want both. Columns change every year based on demand and staffing, so you won't know the exact layout until close to the deadline. A few schools (Broughton in Edinburgh, Mearns Academy in Aberdeenshire) use free choice instead, where you list your preferred subjects and the school builds the timetable around demand.

This is the most important subject choice you'll make. Your Highers determine which university courses you can apply for, and unlike A Levels in England, you sit them in one year with no second chance at the same sitting.

## How many Highers do you actually need?

This depends entirely on where you want to go and what you want to study.

- **5 Highers** — needed for the most competitive courses at ancient universities (Edinburgh, Glasgow, St Andrews, Aberdeen). Medicine, Law, and Engineering at Edinburgh typically ask for AAABB or higher.
- **4 Highers** — enough for most courses at most universities. Strathclyde, Dundee, Heriot-Watt, Stirling, and all modern universities accept 4 Highers for the majority of programmes.
- **3 Highers** — gets you into more universities than you might think. GCU accepts 3 Highers (CCC–BCC) for Nursing. Abertay accepts 3 Highers (ABB–BBC) for most programmes. UWS accepts 3 Highers (BBC–BCC) for many courses.

### The widening access factor

If you're from an SIMD20 postcode (the 20% most deprived areas in Scotland), or you're care experienced, a young carer, or estranged from your family, entry requirements drop significantly.

- Aberdeen offers guaranteed unconditional places at BBC.
- Glasgow guarantees adjusted offers at BBBB.
- Even Edinburgh and St Andrews have Gateway programmes at BBBB.

## Which subjects should you choose?

There are three things to balance: what you need, what you enjoy, and what you're good at.

### Check the entry requirements first

If you have a career or course in mind, check what Highers are required. Some subjects are non-negotiable: Medicine needs Chemistry and Biology. Engineering needs Maths and Physics. Nursing usually requires a science plus English. If you don't know what you want to do yet, keep your options open — English, Maths, and a science, combined with two others, opens the most doors.

### The National 5 to Higher jump is real

Students who achieved an A at National 5 have a strong chance of passing the corresponding Higher. Students who achieved a C at National 5 have a significantly lower pass rate — in some subjects, fewer than 30% pass. Be honest about your grades when deciding whether to continue a subject.

### Crash Highers

Crash Highers — taking a Higher without the National 5 — are more common in S6 than S5. Psychology, Philosophy, and Sociology are the most commonly crashed subjects because they don't have National 5 prerequisites. Crashing Maths, a science, or a language is very difficult and generally not recommended. See our [crash Higher guide](/blog/what-is-a-crash-higher) for the detail.

### The column problem

The column system is the biggest frustration in Scottish subject choices. If two subjects you want are in the same column, you can't take both. Talk to your guidance teacher early, consider S6 for the subject you can't fit, and check consortium arrangements with neighbouring schools.

### Foundation Apprenticeships

Foundation Apprenticeships are available in S5 and S6 in 12 subjects. They count as a Higher (SCQF Level 6), are accepted by all Scottish universities, and Glasgow treats them as equivalent to a Higher at grade A. They're particularly valuable if you learn better through practical work.

### Plan S5 and S6 together

What you do in S5 sets up what's possible in S6. If you want Advanced Highers, you'll need an A or B at Higher. Advanced Highers are critical for competitive courses at Edinburgh and St Andrews, but not essential for most other universities.

## A step-by-step approach

1. **Start with the end in mind.** Look up the entry requirements for the courses you're considering before picking subjects.
2. **If you don't know, keep it broad.** English, Maths, and a science cover almost every degree.
3. **Be honest about your N5 grades.** A C at N5 is a warning sign for the same subject at Higher.
4. **Check the columns.** A perfect plan that breaks the column system isn't a plan.
5. **Think about S6 now.** S5 sets up Advanced Highers, crash Highers, and resits in S6.
6. **Talk to people.** Your guidance teacher, subject teachers, parents, and people doing the courses you're interested in.
7. **Don't panic about columns.** There's almost always a workable combination.

## What to do next

1. **List 5–10 courses you're considering.** Look up the published Higher requirements for each.
2. **Map the requirements onto your N5 results.** Where are the gaps?
3. **Take widening access seriously.** If you qualify, the conversation changes.

S5 is intense, but the goal isn't perfection. It's a balanced set of subjects that opens the doors you actually want to walk through.
