A **Foundation Apprenticeship (FA)** is a real Higher-level qualification you take in S5 or S6 alongside your other subjects. It sits at SCQF Level 6 — the same level as a Higher — combines classroom learning at college with real workplace experience, and is accepted by every Scottish university.

It takes one or two years. It is free. And it counts in your university application as one of your Higher-level qualifications.

This guide covers the 12 main subjects, exactly how it fits into your week, and how individual universities treat them in their offers.

## The 12 subjects available at Level 6

The current Skills Development Scotland framework offers Foundation Apprenticeships in 12 subjects at SCQF Level 6:

- **Accountancy**
- **Business Skills**
- **Civil Engineering**
- **Creative and Digital Media**
- **Engineering**
- **Financial Services**
- **Food and Drink Technologies**
- **Hardware and System Support**
- **Scientific Technologies**
- **Social Services: Children and Young People**
- **Social Services: Healthcare**
- **Software Development**

There are also three pilot subjects at SCQF Levels 4 and 5:

- **Automotive**
- **Construction**
- **Hospitality**

Availability varies by region — not every college offers every framework. Check [apprenticeships.scot](https://www.apprenticeships.scot/become-an-apprentice/foundation-apprenticeships/) for what's running near you.

## How an FA fits into your week

A Foundation Apprenticeship **replaces one Higher** in your timetable. You don't add it on top of five other subjects — you swap it for one of them.

A typical week:

- **1 to 2 days per week** at college and with your employer
- **The remaining days** at school doing your other subjects

Across the year you complete three components:

1. **A National Certificate or NPA** — the classroom-based academic component, delivered at college
2. **SVQ units** — the workplace-based competency assessments
3. **A real work placement** with a local employer relevant to the framework

It's not unpaid work experience and it's not a leisure project. It's a qualification with real assessment standards, issued by Qualifications Scotland (formerly the SQA).

## How universities treat Foundation Apprenticeships

Every Scottish university accepts FAs. The wording varies by institution but the principle is the same: an FA at Level 6 counts as one Higher.

| University | How they treat the FA |
|------------|------------------------|
| **University of Glasgow** | Equivalent to a Higher at grade A |
| **University of Edinburgh** | Equivalent to a Higher at grade A (alongside at least 3 Highers; cannot replace a specifically required subject) |
| **Strathclyde** | Counts as one Higher; particularly valued for engineering and business |
| **Heriot-Watt** | Counts as one Higher; valued for engineering and computing |
| **Edinburgh Napier, GCU, RGU, UWS, Abertay** | All count as one Higher; some courses actively prefer FA applicants |
| **St Andrews, Aberdeen, Dundee, Stirling** | All accept; specifics vary by course |

The important caveat at Glasgow and Edinburgh: an FA cannot **replace** a Higher that's a stated subject requirement for a specific course. If a course requires Higher Maths, you still need Higher Maths — an FA won't substitute for it. But the FA can be one of the other Highers in your offer.

## Who should consider a Foundation Apprenticeship?

An FA is a strong choice if you:

- **Learn better by doing.** The workplace component is real, not simulated.
- **Already know which industry interests you.** An FA in Software Development is much more valuable to you if you actually want to be a developer.
- **Want to strengthen a university application** for a vocational degree (engineering, computing, social work, childcare, accountancy).
- **Are considering an apprenticeship** rather than university — the FA is the natural first rung of the ladder.
- **Want a backup plan.** Even if you choose university, an FA is a valuable CV item and a real qualification you can fall back on.

## Who should think twice?

An FA is probably not for you if you:

- **Need a specific Higher you'd be replacing.** If you want Medicine and need Higher Chemistry, don't drop Chemistry to take an FA.
- **Are already stretched with five Highers.** If you're a strong academic student aiming for the most competitive courses, the marginal Higher may serve you better than the FA.
- **Don't have a relevant FA at your school.** Travel logistics matter — if it would mean an hour each way to the nearest college, it might not be practical.

## The full apprenticeship ladder

The Foundation Apprenticeship is the school-stage rung of a longer ladder.

| Stage | Where | Status | Funding |
|-------|-------|--------|---------|
| **Foundation Apprenticeship** | School + college | Still a school student | Free |
| **Modern Apprenticeship** | Employer | Employed, paid a salary | Free to apprentice — no age limit, 80+ frameworks |
| **Graduate Apprenticeship** | Employer + university | Employed, full degree | Tuition funded by SAAS |

You can go from school (FA) to fully employed degree-holder (Graduate Apprenticeship) without ever paying tuition fees and while earning a salary the entire time. Several thousand Scottish students do exactly this every year.

## How to apply

1. **Talk to your guidance teacher** in S3 or S4 — applications usually open in autumn of S4.
2. **Check what's available locally** at [apprenticeships.scot](https://www.apprenticeships.scot/become-an-apprentice/foundation-apprenticeships/).
3. **Pick the FA as one of your S5 / S6 subject choices** during your school's annual options process.
4. **Apply through your school.** The deadline is typically February to March, the same as other subject choices.

If your school hasn't mentioned FAs in your options conversation, raise it yourself. Some schools are more proactive than others about offering them.

## What to do next

1. **Find out which FAs your school offers.** Ask your guidance teacher specifically — don't wait for them to bring it up.
2. **Match the framework to a sector you actually want to work in.** An FA you're interested in is far more valuable than a "prestigious" one you're not.
3. **Confirm your other Highers will still meet course requirements.** Don't drop a required subject to make space for an FA.
4. **Apply early.** Popular frameworks fill quickly.

A Foundation Apprenticeship is one of the most underused parts of the Scottish education system. For the right student, it's a free, university-recognised qualification with built-in workplace experience — and the first step on a ladder that can take you to a degree without ever paying tuition.
