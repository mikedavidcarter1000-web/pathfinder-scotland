SAAS -- the Student Awards Agency Scotland -- is the organisation that handles tuition fee payments, bursaries and loans for Scottish students. If you are going to university or college at HE level in Scotland, you need to apply to SAAS. It is not automatic.

The good news: the process is straightforward once you know the steps. The bad news: if you miss the deadline, your funding may not be in place when your course starts. Here is how to get it right.

## Before you start -- what you will need

- Your **National Insurance number** (you get this automatically when you turn 16 -- check any letters from HMRC, or call the National Insurance helpline on 0300 200 3500).
- Your **UK passport number** or birth certificate details.
- Your **bank account details** (a current account in your own name).
- Your **household income information** -- this means your parents' or guardians' income if you are under 25 and classified as a Young Student.

If you do not know your household income, do not panic. You can start the application and add income details later. SAAS will also contact your parents directly to verify.

## Step 1: Create your SAAS account

Go to [saas.gov.uk](https://www.saas.gov.uk/) and create an account. You will need an email address you check regularly -- SAAS sends all correspondence by email, and missing a request for information can delay your funding by weeks.

## Step 2: Start your application

Log in and select "Apply for funding." Choose whether you are applying for full-time or part-time study. Select your university and course from the dropdown lists. If your course does not appear, your university may not have confirmed it with SAAS yet -- contact the university admissions office.

## Step 3: Provide your personal details

Enter your name, date of birth, address, National Insurance number and passport or birth certificate details. If you have changed your name, you will need to provide evidence (such as a deed poll or marriage certificate).

## Step 4: Declare your household income

If you are a **Young Student** (under 25, no dependants, not self-supporting for three or more years), your bursary is assessed against your parents' or guardians' income. SAAS will ask for their details and may contact them directly.

The income bands for 2025-26 are:

| Household income | Bursary (non-repayable) | Loan | Total support |
|---|---|---|---|
| £0 to £20,999 | £2,000 | £9,400 | £11,400 |
| £21,000 to £23,999 | £1,125 | £9,400 | £10,525 |
| £24,000 to £33,999 | £500 | £9,400 | £9,900 |
| £34,000 and above | £0 | £8,400 | £8,400 |

The bursary is a grant -- you do not pay it back. The loan is repayable, but only once you are earning above £31,395 per year (Plan 4 repayment threshold), at a rate of 9 per cent of income above that threshold.

If you are an **Independent Student** (over 25, or with dependants, or self-supporting for three or more years), different income bands apply. The maximum support is the same but the bursary component differs. Contact SAAS or see saas.gov.uk for the current independent student income tables.

## Step 5: Check for additional support

### Lone Parents' Grant

If you are a lone parent, you may be eligible for the Lone Parents' Grant (up to £1,305 per year, non-repayable) and the Lone Parents' Childcare Grant (up to £1,215 per year towards registered childcare costs).

### Disabled Students' Allowance

If you have a disability, long-term health condition or specific learning difficulty, you can apply for the Disabled Students' Allowance (DSA). This covers specialist equipment, software, non-medical personal help and travel. See the [disability support guide](/support/disability) for more on what is available.

This is separate from your main SAAS application and has its own process -- contact your university's disability service first, then apply through SAAS.

### Care-Experienced Students' Bursary

If you are care-experienced, the Care-Experienced Students' Bursary provides £9,000 per year (£225 per week), non-means-tested. This replaces the standard bursary and is one of the most generous packages in the UK. The upper age limit was removed for 2025-26, and informal kinship care now qualifies.

### Rural and Island Students' Allowance

If you live in a remote or island area and travel or accommodation costs are unusually high, the [Rural and Island Students' Allowance](/support/rural-island) may top up your support. Apply through SAAS after your main application.

## Step 6: Submit and wait

Once you have submitted, SAAS aims to process applications within four to six weeks. If they need more information, they will email you. Check your email regularly, including your spam folder.

## Key deadlines

| Date | Event |
|---|---|
| Early April | Applications open for the following September |
| 30 June | Guarantee deadline -- apply by this date for funding in place by September |
| September | Course start -- funding must be active |
| 31 March | Absolute final deadline for the current academic year |

You can apply after 30 June, but payments may be delayed by four to six weeks. **Returning students must reapply every year** -- use your existing SAAS account.

## Common mistakes to avoid

**Not applying early enough.** Apply as soon as you have a confirmed or conditional offer. You do not need to wait for your results.

**Not providing income evidence.** SAAS cannot process your bursary without income verification. Chase your parents if needed -- it is in your financial interest.

**Not checking your email.** SAAS communicates by email. If they request additional documents and you do not respond, your application stalls.

**Assuming it is automatic.** Even though tuition is free, you must apply. SAAS does not contact you first.

**Forgetting to reapply in year two.** Each academic year requires a fresh application. Log in every March to restart the process.

## What happens to your loan after you graduate?

Your student loan is managed through the Student Loans Company (SLC) on the Scottish Plan 4 repayment terms. You repay 9 per cent of income above £31,395 per year. If you never earn above that threshold, you never repay anything. If you do, repayments are taken automatically through the tax system -- you do not manage them yourself.

Outstanding balances are written off after 35 years from the April after you leave your course, or earlier if you become permanently disabled or die.
