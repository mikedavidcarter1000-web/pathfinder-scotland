Work-life balance is the second most common reason for changing jobs in Scotland — **26 percent** of career changers, behind only pay (30 percent), according to recent Scottish workforce surveys. **43 percent of Scottish professionals** say they would consider quitting if forced back to the office full-time.

If you're thinking about a career change with the freedom to work from home, the good news is that Scotland's funding system genuinely supports retraining adults. The less-good news is that not every "remote career" advert online is what it claims to be.

This is a practical guide to which careers are realistic, how to retrain, and how to pay for it.

## The remote-friendly careers that actually exist for career changers

The shortlist of realistic options for someone retraining into a remote or hybrid career in Scotland.

### Software development

Scotland's tech sector employs over 90,000 people. The average software developer salary is around £37,500. [CodeClan's 16-week course](https://codeclan.com/) is accredited by Qualifications Scotland (formerly SQA) and the standard entry route for adults switching into tech. It's not eligible for SAAS funding, but Professional and Career Development Loans (£300 to £10,000) cover it.

### Cyber security

A high-demand area with skills shortages across Scotland. CompTIA Security+ certification (£300 to £600 per exam) is the standard entry credential. Many roles are hybrid; some senior roles are fully remote.

### Digital marketing

[Google Digital Garage](https://learndigital.withgoogle.com/digitalgarage) and HubSpot Academy offer free certifications. Entry roles often pay £24,000 to £30,000 for someone with a portfolio of personal projects or freelance work.

### Data analysis

CodeClan also runs a 14-week data analytics course. Google's Data Analytics Certificate on Coursera is a low-cost alternative (£25 to £49 per month). Most analyst roles are hybrid.

### Bookkeeping

The AAT qualification can be studied part-time alongside work. Strong demand from small businesses across Scotland, and most bookkeeping work is fully remote.

### Customer service

The lowest barrier to entry. £22,000 to £28,000 starting, no qualifications required, and many fully remote roles with banks, utilities, and tech firms.

## How to retrain — by time commitment

The route depends on how fast you need to be earning again.

### Fastest route — 3 to 6 months

- **CodeClan 16-week Professional Software Development.** Intensive, in-person or hybrid, leads directly into developer roles. Not SAAS-eligible but Professional and Career Development Loans cover it.
- **Google Career Certificates** (3 to 6 months). £25 to £49 per month on Coursera. Recognised by an increasing number of employers.
- **CompTIA certifications** (£300 to £600 per exam). The standard for IT and cyber security.

### Mid-length route — 1 to 2 years

- **HNC Computing.** Available fully online from Glasgow Clyde College. Typical entry: 1 Higher at C plus a National 5. Funded through college bursaries.
- **HNC Business or Accountancy.** Same model. Strong articulation routes into degree Year 2 if you decide to continue.
- **UHI distance learning.** Particularly useful for rural learners.

### Degree route — 3 to 4 years

- **Modern Apprenticeships.** No upper age limit. Free to the apprentice. You earn a salary while training.
- **Graduate Apprenticeships.** Deliver a full honours degree while you work. Tuition funded by SAAS. JPMorgan in Glasgow employs 55 Graduate Apprentices alone.
- **Standard part-time degree.** Slower but flexible. SAAS Part-Time Fee Grant covers fees if you earn under £25,000 per year.

## Funding — what's actually on offer

Scotland has one of the most generous adult retraining funding systems in the UK.

| Funding | Who qualifies | What it covers |
|---------|---------------|----------------|
| **Individual Training Account (ITA)** | Earning under £22,000 | Up to £200/year toward training |
| **SAAS Part-Time Fee Grant** | Earning £25,000 or less | Fees on part-time HE courses |
| **College bursaries** | Most full-time HNC/HND students | Course fees + maintenance |
| **Modern Apprenticeship funding** | Apprentice (any age) | Free to the apprentice — employer pays |
| **Professional and Career Development Loan** | Adults retraining | £300 to £10,000 — for courses not covered by SAAS |

If you're earning under £22,000, the ITA is the easiest first step — 20 minutes online and £200 toward most accredited training providers.

## Practical reality of remote retraining

A few things worth knowing before you commit.

**Broadband.** Same as for any remote work — the [Scottish Broadband Voucher Scheme](https://www.gov.scot/publications/digital-scotland-superfast-broadband-voucher-scheme/) offers up to £5,000 if you're in a rural area without gigabit service.

**Equipment.** Most employers provide a laptop. If you're studying first, a £400 to £700 laptop is enough for any of the courses above.

**Workspace.** A spare room or quiet corner. Studying from a kitchen table while children are home rarely works.

**Be careful about "self-employment disguised as remote work".** Some adverts framed as remote jobs are actually contractor positions with no holiday pay, no sick pay, and no employer pension contributions. Read the small print before signing anything.

## Coworking hubs across Scotland

If working entirely from home doesn't suit you, Scotland has around 165 coworking hubs. The well-known ones:

- **The Melting Pot** (Edinburgh)
- **Impact Hub Inverness**
- **Glasgow Collective**
- **CodeBase** (Edinburgh, Aberdeen, Stirling)
- **The Whisky Bond** (Glasgow)

Day passes typically £15 to £25, monthly memberships £150 to £350.

## What to do next

1. **Decide on a time horizon.** 3 months, 1 year, or 4 years — every other decision flows from this.
2. **Pick a specific role, not a sector.** "Working in tech" is too vague. "Junior front-end developer" is something you can plan for.
3. **Apply for whatever funding you qualify for.** ITA, SAAS Part-Time Fee Grant, and Professional and Career Development Loans don't apply themselves.
4. **Start small.** A £49/month Google certificate can prove to you (and a future employer) that you're serious before you commit to a 16-week course.

The fastest path into a remote-friendly career in Scotland is through digital skills — 3 to 6 months intensive, or 1 to 2 years part-time. Funding is real and supportive. Aim for hybrid rather than fully remote at entry level — you'll learn faster and progress further.
