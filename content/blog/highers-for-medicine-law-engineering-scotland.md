Medicine, Law and Engineering are three of the most searched career pathways for Scottish school leavers. All three have specific subject requirements that you need to know about before making your Higher choices in S4 or S5. Get it wrong, and doors close that are very difficult to reopen.

Here is what every Scottish university requires, including the adjusted offers available through contextual admissions.

## Medicine (MBChB / equivalent)

Medicine is the most demanding entry route in Scotland. Only five Scottish universities offer undergraduate Medicine: Edinburgh, Glasgow, Dundee, Aberdeen and St Andrews (St Andrews offers a three-year pre-clinical BSc followed by clinical training at a partner school).

### Subject requirements

The subject requirements are non-negotiable at every Scottish medical school:

- **Higher Chemistry** -- mandatory everywhere
- **Higher Biology** -- mandatory or strongly preferred (Physics accepted in some cases)
- **Higher Mathematics** or Applications of Mathematics -- required or strongly recommended
- **Higher English** -- required at most schools

Standard offers typically ask for **AAAAB or AAABB** at Higher.

### Contextualised offers

Contextualised minimum offers are significantly lower than the standard offer. At Glasgow, widening access applicants may receive offers as low as BBBC. At Dundee, the Access to Medicine programme (one of the UK's most generous) makes offers to SIMD 1 and 2 applicants that can be three grades below standard. At Aberdeen, the Gateway2Medicine programme offers a supported entry route.

[Check your widening access eligibility on Pathfinder](/widening-access) to see which contextualised offers you may qualify for.

### UCAT

If you are serious about Medicine, you also need to sit the **UCAT** (Universities Clinical Aptitude Test) -- a separate admissions test taken in the summer before your S6 year. Edinburgh and Glasgow use UCAT scores alongside grades to rank applicants. Dundee weights the UCAT less heavily for widening access applicants.

### Work experience

Healthcare work experience is expected but not formally assessed at most Scottish universities. Volunteering in a care home, hospital or community health setting demonstrates commitment and produces material for a stronger personal statement.

## Law (LLB)

Law is more accessible than many people assume. Every Scottish university with a Law school accepts students with a range of Higher subjects -- there is **no mandatory subject requirement** for Law in Scotland.

### Subject recommendations

That said, **Higher English** is very strongly recommended (and effectively essential) at all institutions. Some universities recommend History, Modern Studies or a modern language, but none require them.

### Scottish Law is different from English Law

An important Scotland-specific point: the **LLB in Scotland is a four-year undergraduate degree**, unlike England where Law is typically three years. This means you can enter directly from school without needing an undergraduate degree first -- unlike the Graduate Diploma in Law (GDL) route common in England.

If you want to become a solicitor in Scotland, you need to complete the LLB, then the **Diploma in Professional Legal Practice (DPLP)**, then a two-year traineeship. The Law Society of Scotland regulates the profession.

## Engineering (BEng / MEng)

Engineering is the broadest of the three, covering civil, mechanical, electrical, electronic, chemical, software, aerospace and biomedical engineering. Subject requirements vary by specialism, but the common thread is Mathematics.

### Subject requirements by specialism

| Engineering specialism | Mandatory | Strongly recommended |
|---|---|---|
| Civil engineering | Higher Maths | Higher Physics |
| Mechanical engineering | Higher Maths | Higher Physics |
| Electrical / Electronic | Higher Maths | Higher Physics |
| Chemical engineering | Higher Maths, Higher Chemistry | Higher Physics |
| Software engineering | Higher Maths | Higher Physics or Computing |
| Aerospace | Higher Maths, Higher Physics | Higher Chemistry |
| Biomedical | Higher Maths, Higher Chemistry or Biology | Higher Physics |

Higher Mathematics is mandatory for virtually every engineering degree in Scotland. Physics is required for most physical engineering disciplines. Chemistry is required for chemical engineering.

### Scottish universities for engineering

| University | Standard offer | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| University of Edinburgh | AAAB | Highly competitive; Advanced Higher Maths advantageous |
| University of Glasgow | AABB | Strong MEng programme |
| University of Strathclyde | AABB | One of the UK's largest engineering faculties |
| Heriot-Watt University | AABB | Strong for electronics, robotics |
| University of Aberdeen | AABB | Oil and gas engineering specialism |
| University of Dundee | ABBB | Civil and mechanical |
| Glasgow Caledonian | ABCC | Applied and professional focus |
| Edinburgh Napier | ABCC | Civil, mechanical and computing |
| Robert Gordon | ABCC | Oil and gas, mechanical |

Contextualised offers at Strathclyde can be as low as BBCC for widening access applicants, compared to a standard offer of AABB. Heriot-Watt operates a similar system.

### BEng versus MEng

**Advanced Highers** in Mathematics or Physics strengthen your application but are not required at most institutions. If you are applying to Edinburgh or Glasgow for competitive specialisms, an Advanced Higher in Mathematics is a significant advantage.

The MEng (integrated Masters, typically five years) is the preferred route if you want to achieve Chartered Engineer (CEng) status. The BEng (four years) also leads to CEng but may require additional postgraduate study or professional development. Both are funded by SAAS.

## The critical point: choose your Highers before S5

For all three career paths, the subject choices you make at the end of S4 determine which doors are open. If you drop Higher Chemistry in S4, Medicine is closed. If you drop Higher Maths, most Engineering routes are gone. Law is more forgiving, but dropping English makes it significantly harder.

The safest strategy if you are undecided is to keep at least one science, Mathematics and English through to Higher level. This keeps Medicine, Law and Engineering all on the table, along with most other degree programmes.
