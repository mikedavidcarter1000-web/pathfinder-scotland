Psychology has no single essential Higher. Most programmes don't mandate a specific subject. What matters most is the **BSc vs MA** split.

## BSc vs MA Psychology

### BSc Psychology (Science faculty)

Usually requires at least one science Higher, sometimes two. Glasgow wants two science Highers at B. Essential for **BPS accreditation** and clinical psychology careers.

### MA Psychology (Arts / Social Sciences faculty)

Typically no specific science requirement. More flexible. Still BPS-accredited at most institutions.

The career outcome is broadly the same — the difference is the route in.

## Psychology entry across Scottish universities

### University of Edinburgh — BSc / MA

Standard **AAAA–AAAB**, WA **AAAB–AABB**. Science preferred for BSc.

### University of Glasgow — BSc

Standard **AAABB**, WA **ABBBB** guaranteed. Two Higher sciences at B.

### University of Glasgow — MA

Standard **AAABB**, WA **ABBBB** guaranteed. No specific science.

### University of St Andrews — BSc / MA

Standard **AAAB–AAABB**, WA **AABB–ABBB** or **BBBB** Gateway.

## Helpful Highers (not required, but useful)

- **Psychology** itself
- **Biology** or **Human Biology** — for the science side
- **Maths** — for statistics, which is heavy in every psychology degree
- **English** — for essay writing
- Any social science (Sociology, Modern Studies, Philosophy, RMPS)

## Career reality check

Becoming a chartered or clinical psychologist requires **postgraduate study** — most commonly a **Doctorate in Clinical Psychology** (3 years, funded by NHS Scotland and very competitive).

The undergraduate degree is the first step, not the destination. Around 15–20% of psychology graduates go on to professional psychology training. The rest go into HR, marketing, social work, mental health support, research, teaching, and many other fields.

## What to do next

1. **Decide BSc or MA.** If clinical psychology is the goal, BSc is the safer route.
2. **Check whether your shortlist requires a science.** Glasgow BSc does. Edinburgh BSc prefers it.
3. **Make peace with statistics.** Every psychology degree has a heavy stats component.
