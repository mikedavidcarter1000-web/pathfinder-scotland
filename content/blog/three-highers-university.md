**Yes.** At least 5 Scottish universities routinely accept 3 Highers for degree-level study.

## Universities that accept 3 Highers as standard

### Abertay

**ABB–BBC** (3 Highers) for most programmes including:

- Game Design
- Ethical Hacking
- Computing
- Law
- Sport Science
- Biomedical Science
- Psychology
- Business
- Nursing

### Glasgow Caledonian (GCU)

**BCC** (3 Highers including English) for **Nursing**. **CCC** with widening access. GCU Pathways is available from BB (2 Highers).

### University of the West of Scotland (UWS)

**CCC** (3 Highers) for most programmes with widening access. **CC + WA programme** for eligible applicants. Available across:

- Business
- Computing
- Psychology
- Law
- Creative Industries
- Sport Science
- Social Work
- Engineering

### Edinburgh Napier

**BBC** (3 Highers) for **Nursing**.

## Universities that drop to 3 Highers with widening access

### Aberdeen

**BBC** (3 Highers) guaranteed unconditional for WA students. Covers Arts, Sciences, and Engineering. The most generous WA offer at any ancient Scottish university.

### Heriot-Watt

**BBBC** — effectively 3 strong Highers plus one at C.

### Stirling

**BBBC** — effectively 3 strong Highers plus one at C.

### Dundee

**BBBC–BBBB** for many programmes with WA.

## The universities that don't drop below 4

Edinburgh, Glasgow, and St Andrews don't drop to 3 even with widening access — minimum 4 Highers (BBBB). But **Aberdeen** (the fourth ancient university) offers BBC, which is a meaningful difference if you're choosing between them.

## The college alternative

If your university shortlist won't accept 3 Highers, the college route is the most reliable backdoor:

- **HNC** — 1 year, needs 1–2 Highers. Articulates into **Year 2** of a degree.
- **HND** — 2 years. Articulates into **Year 3** of a degree.

You finish with **the same degree**. The total time is the same as starting in Year 1 — you're just splitting it between college and university.

Strong articulation routes at Abertay, GCU, UWS, RGU, Napier, and increasingly Dundee.

## Quality vs quantity

Universities would rather see **3 Highers at ABB** than **5 Highers at BCDDD**. A pattern of strong grades in fewer subjects is a stronger application than a scatter of weak passes.

If you're realistically going to scrape passes in your fourth and fifth Highers, drop them and put the time into your strongest three. Your application improves, not the other way around.

## What to do next

1. **List the 3 Highers you're most confident in.** That's your foundation.
2. **Check Abertay, GCU, UWS, and Napier** for any course you're interested in. They may already accept your pattern.
3. **Consider the college route** as a deliberate plan, not a fallback. HND to Year 3 of a degree is often the cheapest and most supportive route.
