There are two ways into teaching in Scotland.

- **Primary teaching** — 4–5 Highers (typically AABB–ABBB), Higher English, and **National 5 Maths at grade B** (not C). Interview and PVG check required.
- **Secondary teaching** — degree in your subject first, then a one-year PGDE.

## Primary teaching entry requirements

### University of Glasgow — MEduc Primary

Standard **ABBB** (S5), WA **BBBB** guaranteed with Top-Up. N5 Maths B. 5-year integrated Masters.

### University of Edinburgh — MA Primary Education

Standard **AABB**, WA **ABBB** (Plus Flag). N5 Maths A.

### University of Dundee — MA Education

Standard **AABB**, WA **BBBB**. N5 Maths B.

### University of Stirling — BA Education Primary

Standard **AABB**, WA **BBBB**. SWAP accepted.

### University of Strathclyde — BA Education

Standard **AABB–AAAB**, WA **ABBB–AABB** (MER).

### University of Aberdeen — MA Education

Standard **BBBB**, WA **BBC** guaranteed unconditional. Most generous WA offer in Scotland.

### University of the West of Scotland — BA Education Primary

Standard **AABB**, WA **BBBB**. CC + WA programme also accepted.

## The non-negotiable GTCS requirements

The General Teaching Council for Scotland (GTCS) sets the minimum standards every primary teacher must meet, no matter which university:

- **Higher English** for all teaching routes
- **National 5 Maths at grade B** for primary teaching
- **PVG membership**
- **Fitness to teach health assessment**

The **N5 Maths B** requirement catches most applicants out — a C is **not** enough. If you have a C, you'll need to resit before being accepted.

## The secondary teaching route

For secondary, you don't apply to a teaching degree at 17. The standard route:

1. Take a degree in your teaching subject (Maths, History, English, a science, etc.).
2. Apply for a one-year **PGDE Secondary** in your subject.
3. Probation year on the GTCS Teacher Induction Scheme.

This means **the entry requirements that matter are the entry requirements for your subject degree** — not for teaching itself.

## The teaching bursary

The teaching bursary is **£15,000** for 2025–26 PGDE students in shortage subjects (reduced from £20,000). The 2026–27 amount has not yet been confirmed.

Shortage subjects typically include Maths, Physics, Chemistry, Computing Science, Home Economics, and Modern Languages.

## What to do next

1. **Check your N5 Maths grade.** If it's a C or below, plan a resit.
2. **Decide primary or secondary.** They're completely different routes.
3. **Get classroom experience.** Most universities expect to see it at interview.
