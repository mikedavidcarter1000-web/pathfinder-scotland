### 5. Dentist

- ai_rating_2030_2035: **4**
- ai_rating_2040_2045: **5**
- robotics_rating_2030_2035: **3**
- robotics_rating_2040_2045: **4**

**robotics_description:**
Dentistry combines licensed manual craft (drilling, extractions, restorations) with diagnostic work. CAD/CAM systems for crowns and veneers are already widespread in Scottish dental practices; robotic drilling pilots exist but are not mainstream. By 2030-2035 AI-generated treatment plans and robotic guidance for implants become standard in larger practices. Core chairside work -- patient communication, fine-motor dexterity in a confined oral cavity, complex case judgement -- remains human through both horizons. GDC registration and the requirement for a licensed dentist to sign off treatment anchor the robotics rating below 5 even at mid-career.

## Flagged for review

**1. Healthcare Assistant / Care Worker jump to robotics 5 at 2040-2045.**
This is the highest robotics rating in the sector retrofit. It reflects humanoid deployment in care home settings for routine lifting, transport, and basic social engagement -- an area where Scottish demographics (rapidly ageing population, rural labour shortages, Free Personal Care funding pressure) will probably accelerate deployment. The rating is defensible but will stand out visually.

**2. Dentist robotics at 3/4 might be slightly conservative.**
Dental robotics (Neocis/Yomi, similar platforms) are already FDA-approved for implant placement; UK adoption is slower but accelerating. Could argue 4/5 is fair. Kept at 3/4 for consistency with the UK lag principle.

**3. Pharmacist robotics at 4/5 is the highest non-care-work rating.**
This reflects actual NHS Scotland deployment (robotic dispensing is standard in acute settings). Defensible against evidence.

**4. AI ratings on AHPs (Dietitian, SLT, OT, Optometrist) cluster at 5/6.**
Aligned with Anthropic Economic Index findings that Healthcare Practitioners face ~60% theoretical AI exposure but regulatory anchors hold the mid-career ceiling at 6. Consistent.

**5. No role rated at ai_rating_2040_2045 = 10 except the AI-native ones.**
Correct -- regulated clinical professions anchor at 6 maximum per the rubric's regulatory cap, even for the most exposed roles (Radiographer at 7 is the outlier and reflects specific task-level AI displacement).
