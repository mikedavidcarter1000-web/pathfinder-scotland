# Robotics Rating Rubric

Rate each career role 1-10 on robotics and physical automation impact at two time horizons: 2030-2035 (early career for current S2-S6 students) and 2040-2045 (mid-career). Robotics covers embodied work -- physical presence, manipulation, and action in the world. This is a separate axis from AI rating, which covers cognitive/software automation.

## Rubric anchors (applies to both horizon columns)

- **1 -- No credible robotics path**: Deep human relationships, ethical judgement, unstructured environments. Examples: Psychotherapist, Social Worker, Primary Teacher, Midwife, Counsellor, Youth Worker.
- **2 -- Peripheral robotics support only**: Robotics handles specific subtasks but core work unaffected. Examples: GP, Nurse, Secondary Teacher, Creative Arts practitioners.
- **3 -- Specific subtask automation without role threat**: Examples: Surgeon (surgical robotics augment), Paramedic (patient monitoring and lifting aids), Pharmacist (dispensing automation).
- **4 -- Meaningful task displacement, role persists**: Multiple subtasks automated; role refocuses on high-value human work. Examples: Vet Surgeon, Electrician, Plumber (at mid-career horizon).
- **5 -- Hybrid human-robot workflow standard**: Examples: Warehouse Operative in modern facilities at early career, Hotel Housekeeping in chain hotels, Chef in high-volume chain kitchens.
- **6 -- Significant displacement in structured settings**: Examples: Cleaner in commercial buildings, Shop Assistant, Bank Clerk.
- **7 -- Majority of traditional tasks automated**: Role transforms into supervisor or exception handler. Examples: Checkout Operator, some Manufacturing Operative roles, some Security Guard roles.
- **8 -- Role largely displaced in mature markets**: Examples: Long-haul HGV Driver at mid-career, Agricultural Labourer on large farms.
- **9 -- Role primarily supervisory of robotic systems**: Examples: Autonomous Fleet Coordinator, Modern Factory Supervisor.
- **10 -- Role exists only because of robotics**: Examples: Robotics Technician, Automation Engineer.

## Key principles

1. **Rate deployed capability, not demonstrated capability.** Humanoid robots demonstrated factory work in 2025-2026 but deployed factory work at scale is a 2028-2030 phenomenon.
2. **UK lag.** UK robot density was 101 per 10,000 manufacturing employees in 2024, vs world average 126 and Western European 267. UK deployment runs approximately 2-5 years behind Asia and Germany. Apply modest UK lag in individual ratings.
3. **Regulatory anchors.** Licensed professions (medicine, law, teaching) have human accountability requirements that cap ratings regardless of technical capability.
4. **Two horizons, not three.** The 2030-2035 rating captures transition-phase deployment. The 2040-2045 rating captures mature deployment. No "today" robotics column is used because current robotics impact is overwhelmingly 1-2 across the career dataset.

## Review cadence

Re-review every 18-24 months. Next review target: late 2027. Assumptions subject to change: UK regulatory environment, dexterity breakthrough trajectory, LFP battery cost curves, major policy shocks (UBI, robot tax, etc.).
