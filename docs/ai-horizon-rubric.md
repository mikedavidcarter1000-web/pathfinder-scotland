# AI Horizon Rubric

This rubric extends the existing AI rating (see docs/ai-rating-rubric.md) with one mid-career horizon:

- `ai_rating`: current reality (anchor unchanged; existing rubric applies)
- `ai_rating_2035_2045`: mid-career window for current S2-S6 students

Both use the 1-10 scale defined in docs/ai-rating-rubric.md. The mid-career rating drifts upward from current for most roles.

## Drift guidance

**From current ai_rating to ai_rating_2035_2045:**
- Knowledge-work roles (current rating 5-8): add 2-3 points
- Embodied and relational roles (current rating 1-3): add 0-1 points
- AI-native roles (current rating 9-10): no drift; already at ceiling
- Maximum capped at 10

## Practical guidance

When rating, ask: "is the role still recognisable in its current form in 2035-2045?" If yes, apply 2-3 point drift. If substantially restructured or displaced, apply larger drift. If fundamentally unchanged (embodied, relational, licensed work), apply 0-1 point drift.

## Regulatory anchor

Regulated professions (licensed medicine, licensed legal practice, teaching registration with GTCS) retain human accountability requirements that cap ratings despite capability change. Doctor, Nurse, Teacher, Solicitor, Advocate remain at approximately 5-6 maximum in 2035-2045 even if AI capability notionally exceeds this.

## Why only one horizon for AI

AI has a less staged deployment curve than robotics. For most roles the 2030-2035 and 2040-2045 AI ratings would differ by only 0-1 points, so a single mid-career horizon captures the signal without redundant columns. Robotics has a steeper deployment curve and genuinely benefits from two horizons.

## Review cadence

Re-review every 18-24 months. Next review target: late 2027. Assumptions subject to change: capability trajectory, regulatory response, economic incentives.

See docs/robotics-rating-rubric.md for the companion robotics rubric.
